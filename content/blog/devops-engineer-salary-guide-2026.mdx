## The Platform Engineer Premium

"Platform Engineer" is replacing "DevOps Engineer" at senior levels — and it pays significantly more.

```
Role                        USA Median     India Median
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
DevOps Engineer             $135,000       ₹20L
Senior DevOps Engineer      $165,000       ₹32L
Platform Engineer           $175,000       ₹38L
Senior Platform Engineer    $200,000       ₹52L
Staff Platform Engineer     $230,000+      ₹70L+
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
```

Platform Engineers build **internal developer platforms (IDPs)** — self-service systems that let developers deploy without DevOps involvement. Skills include:

- Backstage (developer portal)
- Crossplane (cloud infrastructure from K8s CRDs)
- ArgoCD / Flux for GitOps
- Service mesh (Istio, Linkerd)
- Custom Kubernetes operators

## How to Negotiate a Higher Salary

### The Framework

```
Step 1: Research — Know the market rate (Levels.fyi, Glassdoor, Blind)
Step 2: Get competing offers — Even one competing offer shifts leverage
Step 3: Never anchor first — Let them make the first offer
Step 4: Counter high — Ask 20–30% above their first offer
Step 5: Negotiate total comp — RSU, signing bonus, PTO, remote flexibility
Step 6: Don't stop at salary — Ask for learning budget, conference time
```

### What to Say

Instead of: *"I'm looking for around $140,000."*

Say: *"I'm currently evaluating several opportunities in the $155,000–$175,000 range. Can you share the full comp package and see if there's alignment?"*

This one phrase shift routinely gets $10–25K more.

### For Indian Candidates: Countering in Domestic Interviews

```
Employer offers: ₹18L
Your target:     ₹25L

Say: "Based on my CKA certification and 4 years of hands-on Kubernetes
production experience, I'm expecting in the ₹24L–₹28L range. Is there
flexibility there? I'm also considering a role with X company at that
band."
```

Always reference certifications, specific tools, and competing offers.

## How to Level Up to Senior in 2 Years

The fastest path from mid-level to senior DevOps/Platform Engineer:

```
Year 1 — Foundation
├── Get CKA certified
├── Build a personal Kubernetes homelab (K3s/minikube)
├── Learn Terraform, write real modules for AWS
├── Contribute to one open-source DevOps project (PRs merged)
└── Set up monitoring stack: Prometheus + Grafana + Loki

Year 2 — Differentiation
├── Get AWS Solutions Architect certified
├── Learn ArgoCD and implement GitOps at work
├── Study cost optimization — present savings to leadership
├── Mentor a junior engineer (signals senior-level thinking)
└── Write technical blog posts / give internal tech talks
```

This track has been validated by engineers across companies like Atlassian, Shopify, and various funded startups.
